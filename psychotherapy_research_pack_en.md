Subtitle: A research pack for structured study, creative translation, and portfolio development

Updated: 2026-06-30

## 0. How to use this pack

This pack is organized as a working research atlas rather than a loose reading list. It has four layers:

1. Core evidence reviews: high-value systematic reviews, meta-analyses, umbrella reviews, and major guidelines.
2. Approach-technique-indication mapping: a compact framework for matching treatment families with practical methods and clinical fit.
3. Everyday presentation patterns: translation from clinical vocabulary into recognizable real-life scenes.
4. Narrative translation: ways to adapt psychotherapy knowledge into story, character, relationship, and theme design.

The recommendation labels used here are working judgments synthesized from recent reviews plus NICE and APA guidance. They are not a direct reproduction of one official GRADE table.

## 1. Core review recommendations

### 1.1 Overall evidence map

- **The efficacy of psychotherapies and pharmacotherapies for mental disorders in adults: an umbrella review and meta-analytic evaluation of recent meta-analyses**
- **Authors / year:** Leichsenring, Steinert, Rabung, Ioannidis, et al., 2022
- **Focus:** Cross-modality psychotherapy overview
- **Why it matters:** This paper works well as a high-level map. Psychotherapy is broadly effective across common adult mental disorders, but different approaches have different strength areas. CBT has the broadest and most stable evidence base; IPT is especially strong for depression; psychodynamic therapy has stable support in depression, anxiety, and personality-related problems; family/systemic work shows sharper strengths in selected populations.

### 1.2 CBT across disorders

- **Cognitive behavior therapy in the treatment of mental disorders: a unified series of meta-analyses**
- **Authors / year:** Cuijpers, Miguel, Ciharova, et al., 2025
- **Focus:** CBT
- **Why it matters:** This is one of the strongest overview papers for CBT as a whole. Core practical techniques include cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation, exposure, behavioral experiments, and problem solving.

### 1.3 IPT across problems

- **Interpersonal Psychotherapy for Mental Health Problems: A Comprehensive Meta-Analysis**
- **Authors / year:** Cuijpers, Donker, Weissman, Ravitz, Cristea, 2016
- **Focus:** IPT
- **Why it matters:** IPT is especially strong when symptoms are tightly linked to grief, role transition, interpersonal conflict, or social disconnection. The evidence is strongest for depression, especially adult and perinatal depression.

### 1.4 Psychodynamic treatment for depression

- **Efficacy and moderators of short-term psychodynamic psychotherapy for depression: a meta-analysis**
- **Authors / year:** Wienicke, Steinert, Guhn, Leichsenring, et al., 2023
- **Focus:** PDT / STDP / STPP
- **Why it matters:** A useful paper for understanding psychodynamic treatment as an evidence-based option in depression, especially for people caught in repetitive relational scripts, blocked emotional expression, or defensive interpersonal patterns.

### 1.5 Family intervention in psychotic disorders

- **Family interventions for relapse prevention in schizophrenia: a systematic review and network meta-analysis**
- **Authors / year:** Rodolico, Bighelli, Avanzato, et al., 2022
- **Focus:** Systemic / family treatment
- **Why it matters:** A key indication area for systemic work. Family psychoeducation, communication training, and relapse-planning work can reduce relapse and rehospitalization risk.

## 2. Core approach-technique-indication map

| Approach | Core practical techniques | Best-fit indications | Recommendation level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CBT / DBT | Cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation, exposure, behavioral experiments, chain analysis, emotion regulation, distress tolerance, opposite action | Depression, GAD, panic, social anxiety, OCD, PTSD; DBT is especially useful for BPD with self-harm or suicide risk | Strong |
| Psychodynamic / STDP | Clarification, confrontation, interpretation, defense analysis, affect focus, relationship-theme tracking, transference / countertransference work | Depression, chronic relationship difficulty, personality functioning problems, repetitive interpersonal patterns, some psychosomatic presentations | Moderate |
| IPT | Role transition work, grief work, interpersonal dispute analysis, communication analysis, rebuilding support, affect naming | Adult depression, perinatal/postpartum depression, mood problems closely tied to relationship events | Strong |
| Systemic / family therapy | Genograms, circular questioning, alliance and boundary work, in-session interaction practice, family psychoeducation, communication training, problem solving | Schizophrenia relapse prevention, adolescent anorexia, externalizing problems, family-involved cases with high conflict | Moderate |

## 3. Everyday translation

### CBT / DBT

- Often fits catastrophic prediction, avoidance, self-negation, reassurance seeking, or emotion-driven impulsive behavior.
- DBT is especially relevant when emotional surges quickly turn into self-harm, impulsive messaging, substance use, or intense shame afterward.

### Psychodynamic treatment

- Often fits people who keep falling for unavailable partners, withdraw at key moments, or repeat the same emotional fate with different people.
- The main work often involves identifying defenses, naming blocked affects, and recognizing recurring relational scripts.

### IPT

- Often fits emotional deterioration after bereavement, breakup, relocation, graduation, marriage, parenthood, or caregiving strain.
- The treatment focus is typically on current relationship function and the key interpersonal event.

### Systemic / family therapy

- Often fits cases where one member becomes the visible symptom-bearer for a whole family system.
- Particularly useful when relapse rises alongside criticism, emotional over-involvement, or chronic alliance problems in the home.

## 5. Narrative translation

Psychotherapy approaches can also work as lenses for storytelling:

- **CBT / DBT:** how distorted prediction becomes lived reality; how emotion hijacks action.
- **Psychodynamic:** how unresolved past conflict disguises itself as present choice.
- **IPT:** how relationship events reorganize the self.
- **Systemic / family:** how symptoms stabilize a group and preserve a hidden order.

Reusable motifs include the scapegoat character, repetitive romance, symptoms as loyalty, role-transition collapse, and high-expressed-emotion families.

## 7. Story hooks

1. **The symptom is the family fuse**
   A girl's panic and mutism are the price of keeping an intergenerational family secret buried.
2. **The protagonist always falls for the same person**
   The repeated object matters less than the repeated early script.
3. **Automatic thoughts rewrite the interface**
   A game's UI is distorted by catastrophic prediction until the player uses exposure and behavioral experiments to recalibrate the world.
4. **After losing the role of being needed**
   A long-term caregiver becomes depressed after the death of the person they cared for because the loss is also a collapse of social role and structure.
